# Purpose of this document

This document is prepared for R&D tax advisors and accountants reviewing *Humble Ventures*' eligibility for the Australian Research and Development Tax Incentive (R&DTI) for FY25-26. It summarises the R&D activities undertaken in developing *Lunastak*, an AI-powered strategic planning product, and provides an overview of the evidence available to support a claim. The document is structured to demonstrate alignment with AusIndustry\'s eligibility requirements for core R&D activities, particularly the \"four ingredients\" test: experimental nature, technical uncertainty, new knowledge generation, and systematic approach.

**Relevant programs and administering bodies:**

- R&D Tax Incentive (jointly administered by AusIndustry and the ATO)

- Industry Innovation and Science Australia (IISA)

**Key public guidance referenced:**

- ATO: R&D Tax Incentive - Eligibility (ato.gov.au)

- AusIndustry: Software Development Activities - Sector Guidance (2024)

- AusIndustry: Machine Learning Case Study (Swanson Reed, Feb 2025)

**Primary goal:**

To provide sufficient context for an advisor to assess claim eligibility,

recommend an appropriate registration approach, and identify any areas requiring further

documentation before lodgement.
