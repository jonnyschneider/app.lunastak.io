# Humble Ventures Decision Stack - Working Session

**Date:** 2025-07-24  
**Context:** Applying the Decision Stack framework to HV positioning work - "eating our own dogfood"

## Session Overview

Following completion of the 4-session positioning discovery, we decided to visualize the strategy using the Decision Stack framework that Martin Eriksson and Jonny teach. This framework structures strategy as: Vision → Strategy → Objectives → Opportunities → Principles.

## Framework Understanding

**Decision Stack Structure:**
- **Vision:** Aspirational but achievable, customer-centric, future-focused ("where are we going?")
- **Strategy:** Coherent set of choices about how to achieve vision ("how will we get there?") 
- **Objectives:** SMART, outcome-focused, balanced ("what matters now?")
- **Opportunities:** Problem opportunity space mapping before jumping to solutions ("what actions do we take?")
- **Principles:** "Even/over" statements that clarify trade-offs and guide decisions ("how do we work?")

## Connection to Positioning Work

The Decision Stack mapped perfectly to our positioning discovery:
- ✅ Clear target market (growth-stage SaaS leadership teams)
- ✅ Value proposition (strategic clarity through expert evaluation + execution partnership)
- ✅ Strategic approach (service flows, "sniff test" entry point, systematic demand generation)
- ✅ Specific objectives (90-day roadmap, success metrics framework)
- ✅ Identified opportunities ("sniff test", partner network, anchor relationships)
- ❓ Need to articulate principles for trade-offs and decision-making

## Working Session Process

### Clarifying Questions & Answers

**Q1: Vision Scope** - HV as business entity or "Jonny Schneider's advisory practice"?  
**A1:** HV as business entity

**Q2: Strategy Specificity** - High-level approach or include specific service flows?  
**A2:** Strategy articulates steps to achieve vision; offerings and service flows fit here, as well as partnership network and sales focus. Strategy is not tactics - need statements providing direction and alignment. Top-down half (Vision/Strategy/Objectives) concludes with direction, bottom-up half (Principles/Opportunities) deals with execution.

**Q3: Time Horizon** - What timeframe to optimize for?  
**A3:** Vision: 3+ years, Strategy and Objectives: 12-18 months (specify for each), Opportunities: 3-12 months (specify each)

**Q4: Trade-offs Focus** - Biggest decision points needing clearer principles?  
**A4:** Cashflow vs. Business Building, Energy Creation vs. Energy Taking, Ease and Safety vs. Meaningful Achievement

### Development Process

**Vision Development:**
- Started with: "To transform how growth-stage SaaS leaders navigate strategic complexity - creating a world where leadership teams have the clarity and execution capability to achieve breakthrough outcomes."
- Refined to: "To be the trusted strategic partner that empowers growth-stage SaaS leadership teams to turn strategic ambiguity into decisive action and measurable outcomes."
- Noted for further refinement (make pithier) once complete structure established

**Strategy Development:**
- Initial draft: "Build systematic demand generation through expert evaluation offerings, develop trusted partnership networks for scalable delivery, and create predictable anchor relationships that provide sustainable foundation for selective high-impact project work."
- Enhanced with additional strategic themes from previous strategic refresh work:
  - A. Stay in the future - differentiate from commodified consulting
  - B. Keep the mojo - invest in wellness, resilience 
  - C. Wear the pants - breakthrough from sub-contracting to direct model
  - D. Pay the bills - steady foundation while exploring higher-yield model

**Objectives Development:**
- Used aspirational 0-100% style for direction (transformational goals)
- Structured around three strategic pillars with measurable outcomes
- Balance between directional/aspirational (correct for this layer) vs. tangible execution (comes at Opportunities layer)

**Opportunities Development:**
- Specific initiatives with 3-12 month timelines
- Each connects to 1+ objectives above
- Clear deliverables and outcomes

**Principles Development:**
- Started with 11 statements across 4 themes (felt overwhelming)
- Refined to 4 clean "even/over" statements, one per theme
- Focused on most critical trade-offs for achieving ideal state and avoiding burnout cycle

## Key Insights from Session

1. **Framework Alignment:** Decision Stack structure perfectly captured the positioning work - nothing was lost in translation
2. **Strategic Voice:** Integration of previous strategic themes (Stay in the future, Keep the mojo, Wear the pants, Pay the bills) added authenticity and transformational direction
3. **Layered Abstraction:** Strategy/Objectives should be directional and aspirational; tangible execution comes at Opportunities layer
4. **Principle Clarity:** Simple, memorable "even/over" statements work best without additional context
5. **Time Horizons:** Different layers operate on different timescales - critical for practical application

## Most Critical Principles Identified

From analysis of positioning constraints and success factors:
1. **Energy-giving partnerships even over higher-margin solo work** - Addresses biggest constraint (burnout cycle from being "whole team")
2. **Strategic clients even over any paying client** - Addresses misalignment causing over-investment in low-value work

These two principles guide the most critical decisions determining success vs. falling back into 3-6 month burnout pattern.

## Final Output

Complete Decision Stack created with:
- Vision (3+ years)
- Strategy with 3 strategic pillars (12-18 months)  
- Objectives with aspirational targets (12-18 months)
- Opportunities with specific initiatives (3-12 months)
- Principles with 4 clean even/over statements

This provides both strategic direction for the business and practical decision-making framework for day-to-day operations.
