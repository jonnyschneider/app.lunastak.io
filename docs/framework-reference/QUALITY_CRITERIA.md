# Decision Stack Quality Criteria

**Source:** Extracted from Humble Ventures Decision Stack working session and real example
**Date Archived:** 2025-12-12
**Purpose:** Reference for generation prompt design and output quality assessment

---

## Framework Layer Definitions

### Vision (3+ years)
- **Purpose:** Aspirational but achievable, customer-centric, future-focused ("where are we going?")
- **Time Horizon:** 3+ years

**Quality Characteristics:**
- Specific and evocative (not vague platitudes)
- Customer/stakeholder-centric (who it serves)
- Future-focused but grounded
- Memorable and inspiring

**Example (Humble Ventures):**
> "To be the trusted strategic partner that empowers growth-stage SaaS leadership teams to turn strategic ambiguity into decisive action and measurable outcomes."

**What makes this work:**
- Specific audience ("growth-stage SaaS leadership teams")
- Transformation stated clearly ("strategic ambiguity → decisive action")
- Outcome-focused ("measurable outcomes")
- Professional but energetic tone

### Strategy (12-18 months)
- **Purpose:** Coherent set of choices about how to achieve vision ("how will we get there?")
- **Time Horizon:** 12-18 months

**Quality Characteristics:**
- Directional and aspirational (not tactical)
- Strategic themes with authentic voice (not generic)
- Provides alignment and direction
- Can include strategic pillars with memorable names

**Example (Humble Ventures):**
> "Solve tomorrow's problems for product leaders with systematic expert evaluation, build partnership delivery model that preserves personal agency and wellness, and create direct client relationships that provide stability and selective high-impact opportunities."

**Strategic Pillars:**
1. Future-Focused Problem Solving (differentiated positioning)
2. Agency-Preserving Scaling (wellness and control)
3. Direct Client Relationships (stability + selective impact)

**What makes this work:**
- Authentic strategic voice ("Stay in the future", "Keep the mojo", "Wear the pants", "Pay the bills")
- Integration of business goals AND personal values
- Three clear themes that reinforce each other
- Specific enough to guide decisions, broad enough to allow tactical flexibility

**Critical Insight:** "Strategic Voice" - authentic themes add energy vs. generic corporate speak

### Objectives (12-18 months)
- **Purpose:** SMART, outcome-focused, balanced ("what matters now?")
- **Time Horizon:** 12-18 months (specify for each)

**Quality Characteristics:**
- Directional and aspirational (transformational goals)
- Outcome-focused, not activity-focused
- Balanced across strategic themes
- 0-100% style acceptable for aspirational direction
- Measurable but not purely tactical

**Example (Humble Ventures - Future-Focused Problem Solving):**
- 100% of engagements address next-order strategic challenges (not operational firefighting)
- Launch "Sniff Test" with 15+ assessments demonstrating unique strategic insight
- 30% of conversations generated through proactive positioning vs. reactive referrals

**What makes this work:**
- Clear outcome targets (100%, 15+, 30%)
- Aspirational but specific
- Connects to strategic pillar
- Measurable for accountability

**Critical Insight:** "Layered Abstraction" - Strategy/Objectives should be directional and aspirational; tangible execution comes at Opportunities layer

### Opportunities/Initiatives (3-12 months)
- **Purpose:** Problem opportunity space mapping before jumping to solutions ("what actions do we take?")
- **Time Horizon:** 3-12 months (specify for each)

**Quality Characteristics:**
- Specific initiatives with clear timelines
- Deliverable-focused
- Connects to 1+ objectives above
- Practical and executable

**Example (Humble Ventures):**
- **Launch "Sniff Test" offering** (3 months) → Package, price <$1k, run 3-5 pilots
- **Content positioning shift** (6 months) → Growth-stage SaaS leadership focus, next-order challenges
- **Partner network mapping** (6 months) → Identify 8-10 established freelancers via Airtree/agencies

**What makes this work:**
- Clear action verbs (Launch, Shift, Identify)
- Specific deliverables (Package, price <$1k, run 3-5 pilots)
- Timeframes specified
- Connects back to objectives and strategy

### Principles
- **Purpose:** "Even/over" statements that clarify trade-offs and guide decisions ("how do we work?")

**Quality Characteristics:**
- Simple, memorable format
- Addresses real trade-offs in the business
- 4-6 principles maximum (avoid overwhelming)
- No additional context needed (self-explanatory)

**Example (Humble Ventures):**
1. **Strategic clients** even over any paying client
2. **Energy-giving partnerships** even over higher-margin solo work
3. **Own paper** even over guaranteed work
4. **Boundary adherence** even over client accommodation

**What makes this work:**
- Clear trade-offs (not platitudes)
- Addresses actual decision points and constraints
- Guides day-to-day operations
- Prevents falling back into problematic patterns

**Critical Insight:** "Principle Clarity" - Simple, memorable "even/over" statements work best

## Overall Quality Principles

### What Makes Output "Energetic" vs. "Wooden"

**Energetic characteristics:**
1. **Authentic voice** - sounds like how the leader actually talks
2. **Strategic themes** - memorable phrases that capture the "why" (e.g., "Stay in the future", "Keep the mojo")
3. **Specific and evocative** - concrete details vs. abstract concepts
4. **Transformation-focused** - clear "from → to" journey
5. **Balanced aspiration** - inspiring but grounded in reality
6. **Outcome-oriented** - focuses on results, not activities

**Wooden characteristics (avoid):**
1. Generic corporate speak
2. Abstract platitudes ("best-in-class", "world-class", "innovative")
3. Activity-focused instead of outcome-focused
4. Vague timelines or no specificity
5. No authentic voice or strategic themes
6. Sounds like it could apply to any company

### Development Process Insights

From the working session:
1. **Iteration is normal** - Vision refined from initial draft, principles went from 11 → 4
2. **Integration of context** - Prior strategic themes enriched the Strategy statement
3. **Time horizons matter** - Different layers operate on different timescales
4. **Layered abstraction** - Top-down (Vision/Strategy/Objectives) for direction, bottom-up (Principles/Opportunities) for execution
5. **Principles address constraints** - The most critical principles guide decisions that prevent failure patterns

## Product Intent (From Original Vision)

**What it is:**
- "Amuse bouche for strategy development" - warmup, getting started
- Lead magnet that delivers value
- Teaching tool for Decision Stack framework

**What it isn't:**
- "Automated Strategy" (important distinction)
- Deep dive into specific details
- Replacement for strategic thinking

**UX Philosophy:**
- Ask initial open-ended questions
- Clarifying, probing questions for keystone details
- Present visual model with parts completed
- Show thinking, provide critique, suggest iteration paths
